## <a href=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Time>Time</a> 
<font size=3>
Time is the continued sequence of existence and events that occurs in an apparently irreversible succession from the past, through the present, into the future. ã€€It is a component quantity of various measurements used to sequence events, to compare the duration of events or the intervals between them, and to quantify rates of change of quantities in material reality or in the conscious experience. ã€€Time is often referred to as a fourth dimension, along with three spatial dimensions. ã€€Time has long been an important subject of study in religion, philosophy, and science, but defining it in a manner applicable to all fields without circularity has consistently eluded scholars. ã€€Nevertheless, diverse fields such as business, industry, sports, the sciences, and the addressed by general relativity with respect to events in spacetime. ã€€Examples of events are the collision of two particles, the explosion of a supernova, or the arrival of a rocket ship. ã€€Every event can be assigned four numbers representing its time and position (the event's coordinates). ã€€However, the numerical values are different for different observers. ã€€In general relativity, the same for all observers, as first publicly demonstrated by Michelson and Morley. ã€€General relativity does not address the nature of time for extremely small intervals where quantum mechanics holds. ã€€At this time, there is no generally accepted theory of quantum general relativity. ã€€Time is one of the seven fundamental physical quantities in both the International System of Units (SI) and ã€€Investigations into the relationship between space and time led physicists to define the spacetime continuum. ã€€General relativity is the primary framework for understanding how spacetime works. ã€€Through advances in both theoretical and experimental investigations of spacetime, it has been shown that time can be distorted and dilated, particularly at the edges of black holes. ã€€Temporal measurement has occupied scientists and technologists and was a prime motivation in navigation and astronomy. ã€€Periodic events and periodic motion have long served as standards for units of time. ã€€Examples include the apparent motion of the sun across the sky, the phases of the moon, and the swing of a pendulum. ã€€Time is also of significant social importance, having economic value ("time ## <a href=https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Well>Well</a> 
<font size=3>
A well is an excavation or structure created in the ground by digging, driving, or drilling to access liquid resources, usually water. ã€€The oldest and most common kind of well is a water well, to
